Scientific foundations
  • Holland (1997) — RIASEC vocational interest typology; congruence–satisfaction r = 0.20–0.30 across meta-analyses.
  • Prediger (1982) — Reduction of RIASEC to two orthogonal axes: People–Things and Data–Ideas.
  • Spokane & Cruza-Guet (2005) — Meta-analysis of person–environment fit; interest congruence predicts tenure and satisfaction.
  • Barrick & Mount (1991) — Big Five meta-analysis; Conscientiousness r = 0.31 for job performance across all occupations.
  • Schmidt & Hunter (1998) — Validity of selection methods; interest inventories r = 0.10–0.20 for performance, r = 0.20–0.30 for satisfaction.
  • Deci & Ryan (2000) — Self-Determination Theory; autonomy, competence and relatedness need satisfaction predicts job well-being r = 0.40–0.55.
  • Schein (1990) — Career Anchors framework; eight motivational self-concepts predict long-run career choices under constraint.
  • Van den Broeck et al. (2016) — SDT need satisfaction meta-analysis (n > 100,000); autonomous motivation predicts engagement and reduces burnout.
  • Rounds & Armstrong (2005) — O*NET work values predict job satisfaction incrementally above interests; Independence dimension most predictive in knowledge work.
  • Nye et al. (2012) — Interest validity for career outcomes rivals personality when range restriction is corrected.
  • Christian et al. (2010) — Situational judgement tests provide incremental validity over personality for predicting work behaviour.
